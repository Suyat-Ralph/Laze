1. Introduction

1.1 Project Summary

Laze is an application containing a live and dynamic GPS map overview of Wilfrid Laurier University. It allows users to pin points of interest, such as study spaces, restaurant lines, parking spots, etc - all of which are visible to all other users of the application. These pins enable users to view indicated information such as the length of a line or the availability of a parking spot nearby. Users have the ability to support the validity of an active pin or flag to decrease its time before expiry.

1.2 Purpose

This document covers all of the requirements needed for Laze and describes in depth its features and how they work.

The intended audience for this document are the client and the rest of the project team. This document is used by the project team as a guideline.

1.3 Scope and Business Model

1.3.1 Scope

This document demonstrates the properties and constraints of Laze, an application which shows users publicly posted live points of interest around Wilfrid Laurier University campus. Laze aims to provide useful live information free and quickly to all users.  

1.3.2 Business Model

Laze is a free to use application. There are no costs for users downloading the application or viewing it on their web platforms. Our value proposition is to provide useful information to as many users as possible so we don’t want to deter new users behind a paywall. Laze is also void of any annoying advertisements or popups. Instead, revenues are generated by sponsored promotions in the form of pins and live feed updates of small businesses and whomever wishes to promote an event on campus. These groups can purchase a highlighted/top spot for a set duration of time.

1.3.3 Business Example

Users have the ability to highlight a certain post they make on any of the corresponding tabs they choose to post in. For example, in the food tab, businesses such as Mozy’s Shawarma have the ability to pay a one time fee to list a promotional lunch offer that they are running. This helps them maximize user visibility to allow for exclusive promotional offers to be recognized.

2. Overall description

2.1 Product perspective

Laze is a web and mobile application that is comprised of two major components, the front-end and the back-end. Students who use this app can input points of interest (such as open study spaces, restaurant lines, parking spots etc.) which can be viewed by other students. There is no need to install any prerequisite software to use this application .

2.2 Product Functions/Functional Requirements

The users of Laze have the ability to notify other users of specific information about study spaces as well as places to get food. They will be notified of this information through the map. In addiction Laze has the ability to filter out irrelevant pins that are not useful to the user.



2.3 User Characteristics

The intended audience of Laze are those looking for more information about Wilfrid Laurier University campus and surrounding areas. Users are expected to have a basic understanding of desktop and mobile application usage, but if they require further assistance, our information icons throughout our pages can guide them through it. Previous use of apps such as Google Maps, Uber, and Waze will assist in the amount of time required to become familiar with our application. The user-interface is intended to be simple and more importantly, intuitive. Registration, pin placement and commenting on Laze is similar to other applications.

2.4 Constraints

The website allows multiple users to access it at once. The users must also have access to google maps through the internet, because if google maps is down then the app will not be able to function as intended. There is also the development constraint of time, as this project is on a schedule and as  everyone working on this project is a student, there may be severe time constraints when attempting to meet project goals on schedule.

2.5 Assumptions and dependencies

It is assumed that users have their location settings enabled and have accepted the terms of service. Laze’s terms of service indicates to users that misuse of the service is strictly prohibited and will result in banning of all accounts. Violations will include pinning of false information as well as malicious use of our data for third party gain. Any attempts to alter, hack or collude with our software is also strictly prohibited.

3. Technical Requirements

3.1 External Interfaces

  • What are our other dependencies? I.e. are we relying on Google GPS coordinates?
  • What is our source for the live data? I.e. if a user wants to see the line at Tims, how will this be captured?
  • If it is being captured via live photos being uploaded by other users, do we need to consider privacy concerns?
  • Will we be caching any resource or is it all real-time?
  • What is our error logging mechanism? I.e. if we log onto laurier and the site is down, it displays the error message...so do we have defined error codes? (for example, 401 page not found, 404 not authorized, etc)

3.2 Performance Requirements

  • How many concurrent users do we have?
  • What is our baseline? I.e. if we have 5k users @ a time, how will our application response time be?
  • What is the availability of our app? Is it 24/7 or is there a downtime for maintenance?
  • Resilience and robustness? I.e. if we load our app on one server only and it crashes, what is our disaster recovery?
  • Aka. backup plan

3.3 Database Logical Requirements

  • What kind of database are we building? I.e. sequel, no-sequel, Hadoop, etc
  • Is our DB read only or write as well?

3.4 Security

  • How secure is our application? I.e are we using HTTPS protocols?
  • What is our transaction traceability?
  • How are we writing logs?
  • Logs shouldn’t capture any sensitive information related to the users for security reasons

3.5 Portability

  • What is the compatibility of our application? I.e. is the browser/mobile compatible?
  • What are our accessibility standards? I.e. can a handicapped person use our application?
