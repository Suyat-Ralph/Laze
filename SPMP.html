  1. Introduction

 

1.1 Project Overview

Laze is an application containing a live and dynamic GPS map overview of Wilfrid Laurier University. It will allow users to pin points of interest, such as study spaces, restaurant lines, parking spots, etc - all of which will be visible to all other users of the application. These pins will enable users to view indicated information such as the length of a line or the availability of a parking spot nearby. Users will have the ability to support the validity of an active pin or flag to decrease its time before expiry.

  1. Managerial Process

 

3.1 Management Objectives and Priorities

The main objective of this project is to develop an application that will enable users to view an interactive map of the Wilfrid Laurier University Campus with live updating pins containing useful information in many areas of interest. By the end of term (December, 2018) our team goal is to accomplish all project deliverables and have a working version of our application available on web browser, Android, and iOS. Having consistency in our documentation and updating objectives and timelines is at the top of our list of priorities ensuring proper allocation of time and accountability, as there is no budget for this project.

3.2 Monitoring and Controlling Mechanisms

Following lectures on Monday and Wednesday there will be updates communicated through group channels on Slack. Meetings to go over deliverables will take place on an agreed upon time and location on work that requires multiple group members to be present. All accountability and time tracking will be recorded on a Google Doc and submitted in HTML on a minimum weekly basis. Testing and SQA will be completed by non authors promptly following the completion of a deliverable. This is to ensure the team is working diligently to correct any errors before they cause any larger setbacks.
