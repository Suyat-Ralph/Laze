1. Introduction

1.1 Project Summary

Laze is an application containing a live and dynamic GPS map overview of Wilfrid Laurier University. It will allow users to pin points of interest, such as study spaces, restaurant lines, parking spots, etc - all of which will be visible to all other users of the application. These pins will enable users to view indicated information such as the length of a line or the availability of a parking spot nearby. Users will have the ability to support the validity of an active pin or flag to decrease its time before expiry.

1.2 Purpose

This document will cover all of the requirements needed for Laze and will describe in depth its features and how they work.

The intended audience for this document will be the client and the rest of the project team. This document will be used by the project team as a guideline.

1.3 Scope and Business Model

1.3.1 Scope

This document demonstrates the properties and constraints of Laze, an application which shows users publicly posted live points of interest around Wilfrid Laurier University campus. Laze aims to provide useful live information free and quickly to all users.  

1.3.2 Business Model

Laze will be a free to use application. There will be no cost for users downloading the application or viewing it on their web platforms. Our value proposition is to provide useful information to as many users as possible so we don’t want to deter new users behind a paywall. Laze will also be void of any annoying advertisements or popups. Instead revenue generation will be from sponsored promotions in the form of pins and live feed updates of small business or whoever wishes to purchase a highlighted/top spot for a set duration of time.

1.3.3 Business Example

Users will have the ability to highlight a certain post they make on any of the corresponding tabs they choose to post in. For example, in the food tab, businesses such as Mozy’s Shawarma have to ability to pay a one time fee to list a promotional lunch offer they have running on the food tab feed. This will maximize user visibility allow for exclusive promotional offers to be organized as well.

2. Overall description

2.1 Product perspective

Laze is an web and mobile application that is comprised of two major components, the front end and the back end. The student’s who will use this app will input relevant information to the app to determine the relevant icons to display, there will not be a need to install any prerequisite software to use for this application .

2.1.1 Sample GUI


Landing Page Browser


Above is an example of the GUI for our in browser versions. Guests will have the ability to register and create a new account or to enter in their existing credentials to log in.



Similar to our web platform our mobile interface for android and iOS have themes consistent with our brand image.

Main Page Browser



  • In our main page users will see an interactive map of Wilfrid Laurier and surrounding areas. Here, users can view pins and various points of interest such as restaurants and cafes. On the left users will be able to navigate using the highlighted search feature to allow for narrow search results such as “coffee” or “sandwiches”.
  • In the left segment as well are tabs indicating what types of pins are viewable in those specific categories. For example, hitting the study tab will open a drop down list of all available pins to do with study. These can include open study spaces or empty classrooms.
  • Users can zoom in and out of the static map using the + and - icons on the lower right hand side.

These sample GUI’s serve as a basis for our development team to strive for as an end product. Results are expected to vary and changes expected to be made along the way. All of these may be subject to change.


2.2 Product functions / functional requirements

The users of Laze have the ability to notify other user of specific information about study spaces as well as places to get food etc. And they will be notified of this information through the map on Laze supported with google maps. If the user loses connection and is then reconnected they will receive updates on all pins that they may have missed and that are still relevant to them,  in addiction Laze has the ability to filter out irrelevant pins that are not useful to the user.



2.3 User characteristics

The intended audience of Laze are those looking for more information about Wilfrid Laurier University Campus and surrounding areas. Users are expected to have a basic understanding of desktop and mobile application usage, but if they require further assistance, our information icons throughout our pages can guide them through it. Previous use of apps such as Google Maps, Uber, and Waze will assists in the amount of time required to become familiar with our application. The user interface is intended to be simple and more importantly, intuitive. Registration, pin placement and commenting will be no different than other application.


2.4 Constraints

The website has to be able to allow multiple users to access it at once. However if the user is not able to connect to the internet they will not be able to use the app as it relies on back end data to function. The user’s must also be able to have access to google maps through the internet, because if google maps is down then the app will no be able to function as intended. There is also the development constraint of time, as this project is on a schedule and as  everyone working on this project is a student, there may be severe time constraints when attempting to meet project goals on schedule.

2.5 Assumptions and dependencies

It is assumed that users of Laze will have their location permission enabled for use with our apps and acceptance of our terms of service during user profile creation. Laze’s TOS will indicate to users that misuse of the service is strictly prohibited and will result in banning of all accounts. Violations will include pinning of false information as well as malicious use of our data for 3rd party gain. Any attempts to alter, hack or collude with our software is also strictly prohibited.


Laze is also assumed to be functioning on a device with internet web browsing capabilities or application access capabilities. We assume users are familiar with their device’s own capabilities and functions.


3. Technical Requirements

3.1 External Interfaces

 

  • What are our other dependencies? I.e. are we relying on Google GPS coordinates?
  • What is our source for the live data? I.e. if a user wants to see the line at Tims, how will this be captured?
  • If it is being captured via live photos being uploaded by other users, do we need to consider privacy concerns?
  • Will we be caching any resource or is it all real-time?
  • What is our error logging mechanism? I.e. if we log onto laurier and the site is down, it displays the error message...so do we have defined error codes? (for example, 401 page not found, 404 not authorized, etc)

 

3.2 Performance Requirements

 

 

  • How many concurrent users do we have?
  • What is our baseline? I.e. if we have 5k users @ a time, how will our application response time be?
  • What is the availability of our app? Is it 24/7 or is there a downtime for maintenance?
  • Resilience and robustness? I.e. if we load our app on one server only and it crashes, what is our disaster recovery?
  • Aka. backup plan

 

3.3 Database Logical Requirements

 

  • What kind of database are we building? I.e. sequel, no-sequel, Hadoop, etc
  • Is our DB read only or write as well?

 

3.4 Security

 

  • How secure is our application? I.e are we using HTTPS protocols?
  • What is our transaction traceability?
  • How are we writing logs?
  • Logs shouldn’t capture any sensitive information related to the users for security reasons

 

3.5 Portability

    • What is the compatibility of our application? I.e. is the browser/mobile compatible?
  • What are our accessibility standards? I.e. can a handicapped person use our application?
